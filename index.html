Top Tips for the Exam! 💡

1

Read carefully! Always read the whole text before answering questions.

2

Don't panic! If you don't know an answer, move on and come back later.

3

Listen twice! In the listening exam, you hear each part twice. Use both chances!

4

Check your spelling! Small spelling mistakes can cost you points.

5

Practice every day! Even 15 minutes of practice makes a big difference.

6

You can do it! Believe in yourself and stay calm during the exam. 💪

Reading & Writing • Part 3

Reading Part 3: Choose the Right Word! 🔤

📌 What do you do in Part 3?

1

You read a short text (like a story or article)

2

Some lines have a blank space ( _____ )

3

You choose the correct word from 3 options (A, B, or C)

4

There are usually 10 questions

📚 Guided Example

Last summer, my family went to the beach. We swam in the sea and built sandcastles. My little sister found a beautiful shell.

Question 1: Last summer, my family _____ to the beach.

A) went ✓ B) go C) goes

💡 We use past tense because it happened last summer!

💡 Tips for Part 3

  • 🔍 Read the whole sentence before choosing
  • ⏰ Think about the tense (past, present, future)
  • 🔤 Check if you need a noun, verb, or adjective
  • ❌ Cross out answers that are definitely wrong first

Reading & Writing • Part 4

Reading Part 4: Complete the Text! ✍️

📌 What do you do in Part 4?

1

You read a longer text (like a magazine article)

2

There are gaps in the text with a number

3

Below the text you see sentences with missing words

4

You choose the best word from 3 options

💡 Tips for Part 4

  • 📖 Read the title first — it tells you the topic!
  • 🔎 Read the sentences around the gap for context clues
  • 📝 Think about what kind of word is needed (noun? verb? adjective?)
  • 🔁 Read the sentence again with your answer to check it sounds right

📚 Example Story: The Monkey Photographer

🐒

→

Picture 3: Everyone looks at the funny photos

Example story:

One sunny day, a cheeky monkey found a camera on a bench in the jungle. He picked it up and started taking photos of everything — trees, flowers, and his own face! The photos were very funny. When the zookeeper found the camera, he looked at all the silly pictures and laughed and laughed. The monkey was very proud of himself!

✅ Word count: 62 words — Great job!

💡 Writing Tips for Kids

  • 👀 Look at all 3 pictures carefully before you start
  • 📝 Always write more than 20 words
  • 🎨 Add adjectives to make your story more interesting (big, red, exciting!)
  • 🔗 Use connectors: first, then, next, after that, finally
  • ✅ Check your spelling and punctuation at the end
  • 😊 Write a happy or surprising ending!

Listening • Part 3

Listening Part 3: Match the Information! 🔗

📌 What do you do in Part 3?

1

You see a list of people or things (e.g. names of children)

2

You also see a list of options (e.g. activities or places)

3

You listen and match each person to the right option

4

There are always MORE options than you need (some are not used!)

💡 Tips for Listening Part 3

  • 👀 Read all the names and options BEFORE the audio starts
  • ✏️ Write letters lightly (in case you change your mind)
  • 🎯 Listen for the NAME first, then the information
  • 🔄 Remember: you hear everything TWICE. Don't worry if you miss something!
  • ❌ Some options are not used — that's normal!

Listening • Part 4

Listening Part 4: Answer the Questions! ✔️

📌 What do you do in Part 4?

1

You hear a conversation between two people

2

You read 5 multiple choice questions

3

Each question has 3 options (A, B, or C)

4

Choose the best answer for each question

💡 Tips for Listening Part 4

  • 📖 Read the questions BEFORE you listen — it helps you know what to listen for!
  • 🎯 Listen for KEY WORDS in the questions (numbers, names, places)
  • ❗ The answer is not always the first thing you hear — listen until the end!
  • 🚫 Watch out for "distractors" — wrong answers that sound similar!
